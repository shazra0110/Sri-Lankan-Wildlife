Discover Sri Lankan Wildlife

Sri Lanka, an Indian Ocean gem, hosts diverse and endemic wildlife in lush rainforests, national parks, and coastal areas. From elusive leopards
to vibrant birdlife, the island's biodiversity makes it a captivating destination for wildlife enthusiasts and nature lovers.

Biodiversity and Conservation

Sri Lanka's diverse wildlife, like the unique Sri Lankan elephant, highlights its ecological richness. Conservation is vital to protect endemic species and global biodiversity.

Ecotourism and Economic Impact

From leopards in Yala to vibrant birdlife, Sri Lanka's wildlife fuels ecotourism, providing economic growth. Sustainable practices preserve ecosystems and benefit local communities.

Ecosystem Services and Human Well-being

Sri Lanka's wildlife delivers vital services—air and water purification, climate regulation. Preserving habitats is essential for human well-being, maintaining harmony between development and environmental conservation.

Introduction

Explore Sri Lanka's diverse wildlife havens—Sinharaja, Udawala, Kumana—home to leopards, elephants, and unique flora. A nature lover's paradise.

Udawalawa


Udawalawe National Park is a national park on the boundary of Sabaragamuwa and Uva Provinces in Sri Lanka. The park was created to provide a sanctuary for wild animals displaced by the construction of the Udawalawe Reservoir on the Walawe River, as well as to protect the catchment of the reservoir.

Sinharaja Forest Reserve


Sinharaja Forest Reserve is a forest reserve and a biodiversity hotspot in Sri Lanka. It is of international significance and has been designated a Biosphere Reserve and World Heritage Site by UNESCO.

Kumana National Park


Kumana National Park in Sri Lanka is renowned for its avifauna, particularly its large flocks of migratory waterfowl and wading birds. The park is 391 kilometres (243 mi) southeast of Colombo on Sri Lanka's southeastern coast.
