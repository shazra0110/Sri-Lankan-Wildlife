Sri Lankan Leopard

The Sri Lankan leopard is a leopard subspecies native to Sri Lanka. It was first described in 1956 by Sri Lankan zoologist Paules Edward Pieris Deraniyagala.

Leopard Habitats in Sri Lanka

Explore the diverse landscapes where Sri Lankan leopards thrive.

Yala National Park

Located in the southeastern part of the island, Yala National Park is renowned for its rich biodiversity and is a prime habitat for Sri Lankan leopards.

Wilpattu National Park

As the largest national park in Sri Lanka, Wilpattu is home to a significant population of leopards. Its varied ecosystems provide an ideal environment for these magnificent creatures.

Wilpattu National Park

Nestled in the central highlands, Horton Plains is known for its montane grasslands and cloud forests. It serves as another important habitat for the Sri Lankan leopard.

Threats to Sri Lankan Leopards

Human-Wildlife Conflict

Human-leopard conflicts arise due to habitat encroachment. This can lead to leopard attacks on livestock, resulting in retaliatory killings. Conservation efforts include community awareness and the implementation of protective measures.

Illegal Wildlife Trade

Leopards face threats from illegal wildlife trade for their skins and body parts. Conservation initiatives involve law enforcement, public education, and support for anti-poaching efforts.
