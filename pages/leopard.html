Sri Lankan Leopard

The Sri Lankan leopard (Panthera pardus kotiya) is a magnificent big cat species native to the island of Sri Lanka. Renowned for its distinctive golden-yellow coat adorned with dark rosettes, this elusive carnivore inhabits a variety of habitats, from dense jungles to dry grasslands. Classified as endangered, the Sri Lankan leopard faces threats such as habitat loss and human-wildlife conflict, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.

Leopard Habitats in Sri Lanka

Explore the diverse landscapes where Sri Lankan leopards thrive.

Yala National Park

Located in the southeastern part of the island, Yala National Park is renowned for its rich biodiversity and is a prime habitat for Sri Lankan leopards.

Wilpattu National Park

As the largest national park in Sri Lanka, Wilpattu is home to a significant population of leopards. Its varied ecosystems provide an ideal environment for these magnificent creatures.
